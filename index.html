Clinical Context

Pathophysiology

Leukemia is a cancer of blood and bone marrow, in which abnormal white blood cells multiply uncontrollably, crowding out healthy red blood cells and platelets.

The 4 Main Classifications

  • AML - Rapid progression; myeloid line.
  • ALL - Rapid progression; lymphoid line.
  • CML - Slow progression; myeloid line.
  • CLL - Slow progression; lymphoid line.

Why Focus on AML?

Acute Myeloid Leukemia (AML) is the most common acute leukemia in adults. Outcomes vary wildly based on cytogenetics.

Advantage: This platform integrates Age, Blast %, Genetics, and Transplant Status to provide personalized survival curves beyond standard staging.
